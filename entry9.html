A Letter to Moira (#96)

Write a letter to a character in your story.

Dear Moira,

I'm not sure whether you'll find this, or if you're still alive since Offred witnessed you at Jezebel’s. This is being written just to say that you are the most courageous individual in this whole story. Your fake illness; tearing down the toilet; threatening Aunt Elizabeth — all of these acts were an example of what it means to give every other Handmaid a spark of hope. Your rebellion against the system (as much as it was) also made the others realize that their oppressors weren't invincible. I understand that they finally did catch up with you and crushed your spirit by making you dance at the club; however, that first act of defiance had significance. It demonstrated how hard it can be to snuff out the basic desire for freedom in humans.

Sincerely,

Mahmoud Atwa

References:

  • Atwood, Margaret. The Handmaid's Tale. Random House, 1986
