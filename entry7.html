Text-to-Text Connections

Connection to The Pedestrian (Ray Bradbury)

Both works illustrate how totalitarian states use enforced conformity to regulate citizens' behavior. In Bradbury's short story, Leonard Mead is arrested for simply walking outside and exhibiting his own individuality. In contrast, Offred lives in a world where a misinterpreted expression or wrong word could lead to her death. Offred, and in similar fashion Leonard, are both trapped minds confined within extremely closely monitored and mentally oppressive worlds.

Connection to "Eve of Destruction" (Song by P.F. Sloan)

The lyrics of this song warn against the possibility of catastrophic collapse on a societal scale which is parallel to "the before" of Atwood's The Handmaid's Tale. All the fear about nuclear war, the degradation of the environment, and the extremities of politics at the moment also exist within the Colonies of Gilead. It was these extremes which were exploited by the Sons of Jacob as a means to violently topple the existing government of Gilead so they may create a new form of authoritarian rule, and ultimately destroy all remaining elements of democracy in Gilead.

Connection to 12 Angry Men (Film)

In stark contrast to this film that emphasizes preserving functional democracies, the need for fair systems, and standing for your beliefs, even when it goes against the opinions of most people (Juror #8), The Handmaid's Tale illustrates what happens after such systems have been destroyed entirely. Because of the demolition of all forms of justice, Gilead has evolved into a totalitarian society with no jury trials, debate, nor presumption of innocence — only state sanctioned violence (seen in Particicution).
