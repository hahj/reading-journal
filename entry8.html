Believability of Gilead (#60)

What parts of the book seem most believable or unbelievable? Why?

The least unbelievable element in the novel is the gradual nature of the takeover. Rather than happening all at one time, Offred recounts that the government initially suspended the U.S. Constitution using the label of an "emergency" for the country, then froze the bank accounts of women, and eventually fired them from their employment. People did not react with riots to this immediate loss of freedom because they believed it would be temporary. What makes this realistic on a psychological level is that totalitarian governments have taken control of countries through the establishment of a state of emergency which has been gradually normalized as the people lose freedoms, thus being forced into compliance due to both confusion and fear.
