Analysis Using Literary Lenses

Gender Lens

The gender lens clearly indicates that the entire Gileadian society is based on complete domination of women. Gileadians have stripped all financial, property rights, and even reading/writing skills from the women. They have established a color-coded caste system for women with Handmaids being in red, wives in blue, and marthas in green so they cannot unite. This lens highlights how patriarchal societies use systemic control of women's bodies to maintain social order.

Psychological Lens

In addition to the gender lens, the book provides a very effective view of the psychological damage caused by captivity. The psychological harm experienced by Offred while captive can be seen in the way she dissociates and views her body as foreign: "I used to consider my body as an instrument...Now the flesh arranges itself differently. I’m a cloud, congealed about a central object" (Atwood 73). This psychological lens shows that when oppressed people reach a level where they experience such trauma; it will create fractured identity within those who survive such trauma.
